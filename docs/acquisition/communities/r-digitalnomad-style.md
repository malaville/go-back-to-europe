# r/digitalnomad - Community Rules & Tone

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: third-party research (Reddit blocked from automated tools). **TODO: verify at reddit.com/r/digitalnomad sidebar.**
> Size: 2.4M members

## Rules

- **No self-advertisement** (explicitly called out)
- **No market research questions** (surveys, "would you use this?")
- **Photo posts require substance** (trip report or automod answers, no photo dumps)
- **Automod likely removes posts containing external links**
- Standard Reddit spam rules apply (90/10 rule)

## Tone & norms

- First-person experience stories work best ("I was stuck in Bangkok, here's what I learned")
- Practical advice with specific details (costs, visa info, route comparisons)
- "Knowing-how" content outperforms everything (tax strategies, visa hacks, flight booking, cost breakdowns)
- Community values authenticity, hates marketing copy
- Humble-bragging ("working from my beachfront villa") gets destroyed

## What gets downvoted

- Posts where the primary purpose is driving traffic to an external site
- "Check out my app" with no standalone value
- Generic "tips" posts that feel like content marketing
- New accounts with no subreddit history posting links
- AI-generated generic content

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

1. **Do NOT post a "I built a tool" post.** Self-advertisement is explicitly banned. It will be removed.
2. **Frame as a genuine experience post.** "I'm a nomad stuck in Thailand trying to get back to Europe. Here's what I found about routing through Tbilisi vs Istanbul vs China transit..." The post must be valuable even if nobody clicks any link.
3. **Put the link in comments only, and only when asked.** If someone says "how did you find all this?", answer naturally: "I actually ended up building a tool for it because I couldn't find anything that did it, skipthegulf.com"
4. **Lead with the budget angle.** This community cares about costs. "BKK to Paris for €270 via Tbilisi" is more interesting to them than "I built a route finder".
5. **Add nomad-specific tips.** The bus hack (Chiang Mai to Bangkok, 12h, €15, saves hundreds on flights), staying a few days in Tbilisi waiting for better fares, China 144h transit trick.
6. **Check if a weekly discussion thread exists.** Megathreads have looser moderation, safer for a first mention.
7. **Build karma first** if account is thin. Answer 5-10 questions in the sub before posting anything about routes.
