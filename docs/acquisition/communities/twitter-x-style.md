# Twitter/X - Travel Crisis Content Norms

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: web research on viral travel threads, Gulf crisis coverage

## What makes travel threads go viral

- Thread format (5-8 tweets) beats single tweets, 47% better engagement
- First tweet hook is everything (problem statement, urgency, curiosity)
- Visual content: tweets with images have 94% higher retweet rate
- List-based threads ("7 routes to get home from SEA") get bookmarked and shared
- Actionable, curated information (not opinions)

## Best time

- 8-10 AM and 7-9 PM EST see 35% higher engagement
- Early afternoon in Europe + evening in SEA = catches both audiences

## Hashtags (Gulf crisis)

- #GulfAirspaceCrisis (matches CNN framing)
- #StrandedTravelers (AP/Fortune coverage)
- #MiddleEastFlights (broad, high-volume)
- #AirspaceClosure (aviation community)
- #GetHomeSafe (emotional, traveler-centric)
- Airport codes: #DXB #DOH #AUH

## Accounts to tag (1-2 per tweet, never mass-tag)

- @ThePointsGuy (massive travel/aviation audience, covering the crisis)
- @MyLifesATravelMovie (Alyssa Ramos, organized stranded traveler WhatsApp groups, 2,200+ people joined)
- @SimpleFlying (active aviation news)
- @Flightradar24 (providing data everyone cites)
- @RamseyQubein (BBC travel journalist)

## Self-promo norms

- Free tools get amplified enthusiastically IF: actually free, no login wall, lead with value not product, show don't tell (screenshots)
- 80/20 rule: 80% valuable content, 20% promotional
- Crisis context shifts norms: useful free resources welcomed more than usual

## What gets ratio'd

- Treating a crisis as a marketing moment (lead with empathy, not product)
- Mass-tagging influencers in one tweet
- Engagement bait ("like and RT to get access")
- Tool doesn't actually work when people click through
- Not responding to replies
- Text-only when you could show screenshots
- Over-posting (2 quality posts/day max)

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

1. **Thread format, 6 tweets.** Hook (crisis scale) -> Context (why SEA-Europe is broken) -> What you built (screenshot) -> Example route with price -> Social proof (2,200 people in WhatsApp groups, reference Alyssa Ramos) -> Link + "share with anyone stuck"
2. **Lead with empathy, not product.** "21,300 flights cancelled. If you're stuck in SEA trying to get back to Europe..." not "Check out my travel app!"
3. **Screenshot of actual results** in tweet 3 or 4. Show a real route with a real price. This is your proof.
4. **Tag 1-2 accounts max per tweet.** @MyLifesATravelMovie is the highest-value tag (she organized the WhatsApp groups, she'd understand the tool immediately).
5. **Quote-tweet crisis coverage** and add your tool as a resource rather than creating from scratch. Piggyback on existing visibility.
6. **Respond to every reply for 24h.** If someone says "I'm stuck in Manila", reply with an actual route lookup. This generates visible social proof.
7. **No login wall is your superpower.** Emphasize it: "free, no signup, no ads. just put your city + passport."
