# Hacker News (Show HN) - Community Rules & Tone

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: [Show HN guidelines](https://news.ycombinator.com/showhn.html), [HN guidelines](https://news.ycombinator.com/newsguidelines.html), [HN FAQ](https://news.ycombinator.com/newsfaq.html)

## Show HN rules

- Must be something people can **try immediately** (working software, no signup wall)
- Must be something **you personally created**
- Must be **available to discuss** in comments
- Early-stage/unpolished is fine
- NOT allowed: blog posts, signup pages, newsletters, landing pages, minor updates

## Title format

- Must begin with `Show HN:`
- No uppercase, exclamation points, or hyperbolic language
- Be direct and specific, describe what it IS, not a pitch
- Good: `Show HN: SkipTheGulf.com -- Find flights from SEA to Europe avoiding Gulf airspace`
- Bad: `Show HN: The ultimate flight rerouting tool for stranded travelers!`

## Self-promo

- Allowed and expected in Show HN (that's its purpose)
- Account history should not be exclusively self-promotional
- Linking to your own product/site is fine

## Tone & norms

- Factual, direct, technical
- "I built X that does Y" in plain language
- Honest about limitations
- Marketing-speak, hype words, press release tone = flagged/killed
- Developer-heavy audience, allergic to being sold to
- Be genuinely receptive to criticism, never defensive

## Comments

- Post a top-level "Author here" comment immediately
- Respond to every comment thoughtfully
- Technical details are appreciated (stack, architecture, interesting problems solved)
- If someone points out a flaw, acknowledge it
- Never complain about votes or flags

## Anti-manipulation (strict)

- Never ask friends to upvote (detection is excellent, will kill the post)
- No booster comments (community spots fake praise instantly)
- Brand-new accounts may be suppressed
- Don't delete and repost

## Best time

- Tuesday-Wednesday, ~8-9 AM US Eastern (13-14h UTC)

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

1. **Lead with the engineering story, not the product.** HN cares about how you solved it. "I reverse-engineered Google Flights' internal API" is more interesting to them than "find cheap flights".
2. **Post the "Author here" comment within 60 seconds.** Include: why you built it (personal story, stuck in Thailand), stack (Next.js, Neon, Vercel), interesting technical challenge (GFAD API, BFS route graph, visa rule engine), honest limitations.
3. **Welcome criticism as free consulting.** If someone says "your pricing is wrong", reply with "yeah, prices are cached and can drift 10-15%, working on real-time verification". Never defensive.
4. **If someone says "just use Google Flights"**, your answer: "Google Flights doesn't check transit visa requirements by passport nationality, and doesn't filter Gulf airspace routes. That's the gap."
5. **Share the source if asked.** HN trusts open builders. If someone asks to see the code, be ready.
6. **Don't over-engage.** Answer every question once, thoughtfully. Don't reply to replies to replies. Let the community discuss among themselves.
7. **Wait for Tuesday-Wednesday 13-14h UTC.** Do not post on weekend.
