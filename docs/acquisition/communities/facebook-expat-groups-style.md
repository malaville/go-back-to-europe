# Facebook Expat Groups - Community Rules & Tone

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: research across Expats in Bangkok, Brits in Thailand, Digital Nomads Thailand, Backpacking SEA

## Expats in Bangkok - Specific Rules (from admin, verified 2026-03-08)

- **Business promo requires prior arrangement** with management via expatsinbangkok.com/contactus
- **Self-promotion, spam, irrelevant links explicitly banned**
- Posts must be relevant to expat life in Bangkok/Thailand
- "Pointless posts will not be approved" (posts are pre-screened by admins)
- Must have real profile and photo, FB account older than 1 month
- English only
- No copy-paste, must have original text, at least 10 words
- No similar website/group/WhatsApp mentions (they see competitors as threat)
- Respect required, no bullying

**Key risk: "No website, social media page, group, What's App or similar outlet that is similar to ours."** This could be interpreted broadly. skipthegulf.com is not similar to their group (it's a flight tool, not an expat community), but a strict admin might flag it.

## Rules (common across most groups)

- **No unsolicited links** to your own products/services/websites
- **No drive-by posting** (joining just to drop a link = instant ban)
- Some groups have **promo days** (Self-Promo Saturdays, weekly threads)
- Rules in pinned post or "About" section, admins expect you to read them
- **Crisis posts get special treatment** (admins relax promo rules for genuinely stranded people)

## Tone & norms

- Helpful and direct, people are solving real problems (visas, flights, scams)
- Casual but not flippant, real stress involved
- Personal experience carries weight ("I just did this last week")
- Solution-oriented posts get the most engagement
- Don't lecture or be condescending

## Algorithm (critical)

- **External links get 70-80% less reach** than native content
- **Put links in first comment**, not in post body (Meta officially recommends this since May 2025)
- Text-only posts with an image attachment get best reach
- Groups get 30-60% organic reach vs 2-6% for pages

## Best practices

- Join and engage for a day or two before posting (answer 3-5 questions first)
- Post as yourself, not a brand page
- Lead with the problem, not the tool
- Frame as personal experience ("I was trying to figure out...")
- Invite feedback ("would love to know if this is useful")
- Offer to look up routes for individuals (high engagement)
- Screenshot of results as image attachment boosts reach

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

1. **Day 0 (today): Join and help.** Answer 3-5 questions in each group. Flight questions, visa questions, anything. Be useful. Don't mention the tool.
2. **Day 1: Post as someone asking for help, not offering it.** "Is anyone else still trying to figure out routes back to Europe? I've been stuck since March 1..." This frames you as a fellow community member, not an outsider promoting something.
3. **Attach a screenshot of results**, not a link. Text post + image gets best algorithm treatment. Put the URL in first comment only.
4. **End with "drop your situation and I'll look it up".** This turns the post into a service thread. Every reply you give is visible social proof. High comment count boosts the post in the algorithm.
5. **Reply to every comment with a real route lookup.** "You're in Chiang Mai with a German passport? BKK to IST on Turkish for €310 next Thursday, no visa needed for transit." Then "here's the link if you want to check: skipthegulf.com"
6. **Different angle per group.** Expats in Bangkok: BKK-specific routes. Brits in Thailand: UK passport visa traps. French in Asia: France Travail situation, speak French. Don't copy-paste.
7. **If an admin questions it, be transparent.** "Yeah I built it, it's free, no ads, I'm stuck here too. Happy to take it down if it's not helpful." Admins respect honesty.

## What NOT to do

- Don't post same text across multiple groups (admins talk, Facebook flags identical posts)
- Don't use marketing language ("amazing", "game-changer")
- Don't post in 5 groups the same day (space over 2-3 days)
- Don't link to Telegram/WhatsApp groups (instant ban in many groups)
- Don't have a thin/empty Facebook profile (admins review before approving)
