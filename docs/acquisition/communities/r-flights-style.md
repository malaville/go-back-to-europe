# r/flights - Community Rules & Tone

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: observation from posting in megathread

## Rules

- Megathread format for recurring topics (Gulf shutdown, rebookings)
- No promo, advice only in megathread
- Top 1% commenters carry authority, contradicting them without evidence = downvotes

## Tone & norms

- Technical, factual, airline-savvy audience
- Expects specific knowledge (alliance rebooking rules, fare classes, etc.)
- Snarky humor tolerated between regulars, not from outsiders
- "least unhinged [sub] commenter" meme does NOT land when you're the one being corrected

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

**Status: megathread is LOCKED. Can't comment. DMs only.**

1. **Stay in the megathread.** Don't create standalone posts. The mod invited you there, that's your lane.
2. **Be the "helpful route nerd" in comments.** When someone asks "how do I get from X to Y", reply with a real answer (airline, price, date). If they want to know more, they'll ask.
3. **Never contradict Top 1% commenters head-on.** They're the regulars. If they say something you disagree with, frame it as "yeah and also..." not "actually no...".
4. **Show your work.** This is a technical sub. "BKK to IST on TK for €340, checked 10 min ago" lands better than "I built a tool that finds routes".
5. **If someone asks what tool you use**, answer honestly: "I built skipthegulf.com because I was stuck in Thailand". Don't volunteer it unprompted.
6. **Tone: knowledgeable peer, not marketer.** Think frequent flyer forum, not product launch.
