# r/solotravel - Community Rules & Tone

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: third-party research. **TODO: verify at reddit.com/r/solotravel sidebar.**

## Rules

- Posts must be about **solo travel** specifically, not travel in general
- **No low-effort posts or polls** (removed without warning)
- **"Do your own research"** - "plan my trip" posts, anything Google-able = removed
- When asking for advice, include at least 2 of: budget, itinerary, interests, research done
- Search before posting (repetitive questions removed)
- **Zero tolerance on inclusivity** (racism, sexism, gatekeeping = ban. Sex tourism = instant ban)
- Weekly threads for meetups, accommodation, rants, "travel victories"
- Trip reports encouraged (template available)

## Self-promotion

- **No explicit rule banning links** to blogs/YouTube/tools (more permissive than most travel subs)
- Reddit sitewide 90/10 rule still applies
- A link with no context or engagement still treated as spam by community
- Posts must provide standalone value regardless of any link

## Tone & norms

- Authentic, first-person, story-driven
- "Super supportive" community for genuine advice seekers
- Show your work (research done, specific questions)
- Conversational and humble, hostel common room vibe
- Specific over vague, always

## No explicit AI ban

- No written rule, but AI-sounding posts will get downvoted as low-effort
- Must read as genuinely human and experience-driven

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

1. **This is one of the friendlier subs for your use case.** No explicit link ban, supportive community, solo travelers in SEA are the exact audience.
2. **Frame as a solo traveler story.** "I'm solo in Thailand trying to figure out how to get home. Here's what routes are actually working right now..." Share real intel (routes, prices, visa traps).
3. **Include your research.** The community respects people who've done the work. Show specific routes you've checked, prices, what didn't work.
4. **Mention the tool naturally.** "I got frustrated checking combinations manually so I built a thing to automate it" is on-brand for this sub. They appreciate builders who solve their own problems.
5. **Link directly in the post is probably safe** given their permissive link policy, but test the waters with the text first and link in comments if unsure.
6. **Trip report format** could work well: "How I'm planning my route home from SEA during the Gulf closure" as a quasi-trip-report.
7. **End with genuine engagement.** "Anyone else dealing with this? What routes are you finding?" turns it into a discussion, not a pitch.
