# r/Thailand - Community Rules & Tone

> Fetched: 2026-03-08
> Source: Reddit API (rules JSON). Verified.
> Size: ~925,000 subscribers

## Rules (14 rules, strictly enforced, ban without warning possible)

1. **Tourism content banned** - redirected to r/ThailandTourism. Strictly enforced.
2. English or Thai only
3. Standard reddiquette
4. No offensive content/harassment/xenophobia
5. No illegal content (drugs, sex tourism, lese majeste)
6. News from reputable sources only (Thaiger explicitly blacklisted). Original headlines required.
7. **Personal blogs/vlogs/channels/Instagram/Patreon/GoFundMe prohibited**
8. **Spam/self-promotion = ban without warning.** Includes ads, referral links, selling items.
9. Monthly FAQ thread for common questions
10. COVID posts fine if Thailand-relevant
11. Dating/relationship posts removed (go to r/Relationship_Advice)
12. "Serious" flair for heavy moderation
13. No negativity bait or country rivalries
14. **AI content explicitly banned**: "comments copied/pasted from e.g. ChatGPT will be removed"

## Tone & norms

- Skews toward people who LIVE in Thailand, not tourists
- Expat content welcome (visas, cost of living, working)
- Substantive text posts about life, policy, culture
- Photos need meaningful context
- Zero tolerance for anything that smells like promotion

## Strategy: how to play safe and be liked

**This is the hardest sub on the list. Self-promo is a hard ban with instant ban risk.**

1. **Do NOT post about skipthegulf.** Rules 7 and 8 together make any self-promotion a bannable offense. No exceptions.
2. **The only viable path is organic.** Participate genuinely in expat discussions. If someone posts about being stuck (in the sub or monthly FAQ thread), reply with route intel as a helpful comment. No link unless directly asked.
3. **If someone asks how you found the info**, say "I built a thing, happy to DM you the link" instead of posting it publicly. This respects Rule 8 while still being helpful.
4. **Consider r/ThailandTourism instead.** Tourist/travel content is explicitly redirected there. Check their rules separately.
5. **Never sound like ChatGPT.** Rule 14 is explicit. Write like a human, typos and all.
6. **If posting about the Gulf crisis**, frame it as an expat issue ("anyone else stuck here trying to get home?"), not a tourist question. The sub cares about people who live there.
